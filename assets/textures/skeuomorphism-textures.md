# Skeuomorphism Textures

Use these textures as references for materials, not as an instruction to overload every surface.

## Leather

- Base: `linear-gradient(135deg, #5D4037 0%, #3E2723 100%)`
- Detail: subtle repeating grain with low-opacity dark lines
- Best for: premium buttons, notebooks, dark cards

## Brushed Metal

- Base: `linear-gradient(180deg, #546E7A 0%, #37474F 100%)`
- Detail: fine vertical or horizontal highlight streaks
- Best for: toolbars, knobs, utility panels

## Wood

- Base: warm brown gradient from walnut to oak
- Detail: soft grain, not high-contrast photo texture
- Best for: frames, decorative panels, nostalgic containers

## Glossy Plastic

- Base: saturated color gradient from lighter top to darker bottom
- Detail: top-half highlight and rounded reflections
- Best for: playful buttons, retro controls, bright callouts

## Paper

- Base: off-white or cream with tiny warm noise
- Detail: subtle edge shadow and light surface variation
- Best for: notes, cards, vintage documents

## Usage Rules

- Choose one dominant material and one supporting material
- Keep texture scale consistent across the UI
- Prioritize readability over realism
- Test performance if using multiple layered gradients and shadows
